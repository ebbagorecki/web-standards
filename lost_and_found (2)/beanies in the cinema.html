BEANIES IN THE CINEMA

A short story · Starring Margo

••••••••••••••••

Margo loves the cinema. She loves the velvet seats, the trailers, the way the lights dim in slow motion. She loves popcorn at the wrong moments and crying quietly in the dark.

She also loves beanies. Knitted ones, slouchy ones, one with a tiny pom-pom that looks like a cloud. She wears them in, takes them off when the lights go down, and somewhere between the opening credits and the final fade — they vanish.

This page is a small monument. A reel of every beanie that has slipped between the seats and stayed there. May they rest under chair G14, content, in the warm hum of the projector.
